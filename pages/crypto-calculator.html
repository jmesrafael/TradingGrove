Risk Management Fundamentals

Risk 1–2% per trade

Professional traders rarely risk more than 1–2% of their account on a single trade. This keeps drawdowns manageable and lets you survive losing streaks.

Aim for 2R or better

A minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio means you only need a 34% win rate to break even. At 50% win rate with 2R, you grow consistently over time.

Protect your capital first

Capital preservation is the #1 job of a trader. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. Use this calculator every time, no exceptions.

Leverage cuts both ways

High leverage amplifies gains but also losses. Always size your position based on your SL distance — not your account balance alone.

Consistency over perfection

One outsized loss can erase weeks of gains. Consistent 1–2% risk sizing across hundreds of trades compounds into edge — impulsive sizing destroys it.

Track every trade

Knowing your position size is only half the edge. Log every trade to discover which setups actually work for you — patterns emerge fast.

Frequently Asked Questions
Why is calculating risk before every trade so important?
Calculating risk before trading is the #1 habit that separates profitable traders from those who blow up accounts. Without pre-calculated position sizes, traders tend to over-leverage on winning streaks and panic-exit on losses. This calculator enforces discipline by showing you exactly how much you'll lose if stopped out, keeping emotions in check.
What makes this calculator better than others?
This calculator handles leverage automatically, shows your exact margin requirement, calculates risk/reward ratios in real-time, and stores your defaults so you can calculate the same way every time. Most calculators make you do the math manually. This one is designed for speed and consistency, so you can focus on finding better trades.
How does leverage factor into my position size?
Leverage lets you control more value with less capital. 10x leverage means your $100 risk controls a $1000 position. The risk amount stays the same, but you need less margin upfront. This calculator shows your exact margin requirement after factoring in leverage, so you always know if you have enough capital to open the trade.
What's a good risk-to-reward ratio target?
Professional traders typically target 1:2 or better. This means risking $1 to make $2. At a 1:2 ratio with only 34% winning trades, you're still profitable. The higher your ratio, the fewer winners you need. This calculator displays your R:R instantly so you can exit trades that don't meet your minimum standard.
Can I use this for different risk amounts?
Yes. You can toggle between percentage risk (1% of your account) or fixed dollar amounts (like $50 per trade). Percentage mode scales automatically as your account grows. Fixed mode lets you test a specific dollar amount. Most pros use percentage to stay consistent as their account changes.
